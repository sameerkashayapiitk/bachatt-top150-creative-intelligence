Glossary

What every number means.

Every metric, score and label used across this site — with the exact formula, where the number comes from, and the caveats. “From data” = pulled/computed from AppsFlyer + Meta. “Judgment” = a 1–10 read from the transcript & frames (relative, not a Meta metric).

01
Performance metrics

Hard numbers from the ad platforms. AppsFlyer is canonical for installs/CPI; Meta for purchases, CAC, video & engagement.

Spend ₹ from data
Total money spent delivering the creative over the window. When one creative ran as several ads (different placements/accounts), spend is summed across all of them.
How it's calculated: summed from the AppsFlyer×Meta master (AppsFlyer-canonical `af_cost_inr`, native INR per day).
CTR % · link from data
link clicks ÷ impressions × 100
Click-through rate — share of viewers who clicked the link. The cleanest data-backed signal of how convincing the hook + script are. Account median ≈ 0.55%.
How it's calculated: Meta link clicks and impressions, aggregated across the creative's ad instances, then recomputed.
CPI ₹ · per install from data
spend ÷ installs
Cost per app install — what one install cost. Blended ≈ ₹44.
How it's calculated: AppsFlyer-attributed installs ÷ AppsFlyer spend (AppsFlyer is the source of truth for installs).
CAC ₹ · per customer from data
spend ÷ purchases
Customer acquisition cost — what one paying customer cost. ≈ 9× CPI because only ~10% of installs purchase. Shows “—” when the ad has no attributed purchase.
How it's calculated: Meta-attributed purchases (single `omni_purchase`, no double-count) ÷ spend. Blended ≈ ₹412.
⚠ Not the same as CPI — an install is not a customer.
CPM ₹ · per 1,000 from data
spend ÷ impressions × 1,000
Cost to show the ad 1,000 times — the price of reach.
How it's calculated: Meta/AppsFlyer impressions and spend. (Shown in the data, not on every card.)
Video 100% rate % from data
100%-completions ÷ impressions × 100
Share of impressions that watched the video to the very end — a retention/hold signal.
How it's calculated: Meta `video_100pct_plays` ÷ impressions.
02
Creative scores

1–10 reads made while watching each ad (transcript + opening frames). These are qualitative and relative across ads — judgments, not platform metrics. Pair them with CTR (the hard number) on the same card.

Stopping Power 1–10 judgment
How hard the first 0–3s (opening frame `t000s` + first spoken line) stops the scroll. 8–9 = a sharp pattern-interrupt (e.g. “₹2 crore to quit smoking?” on screen); 6 = solid but not a jolt; 3–4 = slow open.
How it's calculated: scored by the analysis model from the opening frame + first transcript line, against a fixed rubric.
⚠ Judgment, not Meta's 3-sec hook-rate (that column was unreliable in this export).
Emotional Intensity 1–10 judgment
Peak emotional charge of the ad — how strongly it makes you feel something at its high point. (In the data; not shown on the card metric line.)
How it's calculated: scored by the model across the full transcript + milestone frames.
03
Classification axes

Every creative is tagged on four axes from its transcript + frames, using a fixed vocabulary (so they aggregate). Each gets a primary and an optional secondary label.

Theme 10 labels
The narrative situation the ad is built around (e.g. Emergency Fund, Aspiration/Dreams, Daily Micro-Savings, Fraud/Loss-Avoidance).
How it's calculated: assigned by the model from the storyline; one primary + optional secondary.
Emotion 9 labels
The dominant feeling the ad leads with — what it makes the viewer feel. The whole site is ranked by which emotion's creatives carry the most spend.
How it's calculated: assigned from the arc + tone; primary drives the scoreboard.
Hook type 8 types
The device used in the first 3 seconds: Problem · Contrarian · Shock · Curiosity · Authority · Financial · Story · Identity.
How it's calculated: assigned from the opening beat.
Frame hook text
A short description of the opening visual that stops the scroll — read straight off the first frame (e.g. “close-up of cash being counted”).
How it's calculated: written from frame `t000s` (0-second mark).

04
Confidence, links & scope
Confidence H / M / L
How trustworthy a creative's labels are. H/M = grounded in the actual transcript + frames. L = name-only inference (no transcript/frames were available, usually a non-Hindi language variant).
How it's calculated: 94 of 141 are H/M; the rest are L and flagged on the card.
▶ Drive link
The source production video file on Google Drive (internal, full-res). Shown only when matched by the ad's `|| NNN ||` creative-ID, so it can't be a fuzzy mismatch — 100 ads.
How it's calculated: from the per-language creative inventory, keyed by creative-ID.
◎ Instagram / Facebook
The live ad creative as it ran on Meta — public permalink. Present for all 141 ads; the only link when no verified Drive file exists.
How it's calculated: fetched from the Meta Graph API by each ad's `ad_id` (140 Instagram, 1 Facebook), so it is authoritative.
⚠ Fetching by ad_id is why these can't be the wrong video.
Scope
Top 150 unique creatives by spend (ad rows deduped to one per creative; 9 non-story Outro/IMG/Referral ads excluded → 141 analysed). Covers ~70% of all ad spend.
How it's calculated: ranked from the AppsFlyer×Meta master. Window: Oct 2025 – Jun 2026 (snapshot 22 Jun 2026).
